Celestia

A Dungeons and Dragons Setting

By Ani Mihaylova

A Brief description of Celestia

The country of Celestia is a deeply religious land, ruled by a powerful theocratic organization known as the Divine Council. The Divine Council is composed of devout followers who believe in the worship of a multitude of deities, each with their own area of influence and power. Their teachings shape every aspect of life in Celestia.

The history of Celestia is marred by the destructive consequences of arcane magic. In the past, arcane practitioners became incredibly powerful, but their unchecked ambition and reckless pursuit of power led to devastating consequences. Their experiments often resulted in catastrophic events, causing widespread destruction and suffering throughout the land.

In response to this crisis, the Divine Council was formed with the primary goal of restoring balance and protecting the innocent from the dangers of arcane magic. The Council, composed of representatives from the eight factions that follow the major deities, united their efforts to confront and subdue the reckless arcane practitioners. Through fierce battles and continuous persecution, the Council was able to overcome the arcane practitioners, either by defeating them in combat, imprisoning them, or banishing them from Celestia.

As a result of this dark period in history, the Divine Council established a strict law prohibiting the practice of arcane magic in Celestia. This law serves as a safeguard to prevent the resurgence of reckless and destructive arcane practices, and to protect the citizens of Celestia from the devastating consequences of uncontrolled magic. The Council continues to uphold and enforce this law, ensuring that the citizens of Celestia are shielded from the risks and dangers associated with arcane magic, and that the realm remains safe and balanced.

Despite the Divine Council's strict rule, Celestia is a prosperous country, with a strong economy and a skilled workforce. The country's vast farmlands and rich mines provide ample resources for the people, and many skilled artisans and craftsmen call Celestia home.

However, not all are content under the Divine Council's rule. Some see the religious order as oppressive and seek to overthrow them, while others simply wish for more personal freedoms. These dissenters often gather in secret, hoping to one day overthrow the Divine Council and create a more egalitarian society.

As adventurers in Celestia, the players may find themselves caught up in the political intrigue of the Divine Council and its dissenters. They may be tasked with carrying out the Divine Council's will, which may involve rooting out practitioners of arcane magic or hunting down rebels. Alternatively, they may choose to join the resistance and fight against the religious order's persecution of arcane magic. Whatever path they choose, the players will find themselves in a land deeply shaped by religion, where faith and politics are deeply intertwined, and arcane magic is viewed with fear and suspicion.

Divine Council Hierarchy: The Divine Council is not a monolithic entity, but rather a complex hierarchy of priests, clerics, and other devout followers. The highest-ranking members of the Divine Council are known as the "Elders," and they hold immense power and authority in the country. They are believed to be the direct conduits of the deities and serve as the ultimate decision-makers in matters of faith and governance. Below the Elders are various branches of the Divine Council, such as the Order of the Sanctum, the Keepers of the Sacred Texts, and the Inquisitors, each with its own responsibilities and influence.

Religious Rites and Ceremonies: Religion plays a central role in the daily lives of Celestia's inhabitants, and the country is known for its elaborate religious rites and ceremonies. From grand processions and festivals that celebrate the different deities, to solemn rituals of purification and atonement, the people of Celestia are deeply committed to their faith. These religious practices are often closely regulated by the Divine Council, and participation is expected from all citizens.

Role of the Church: The Church of Celestia, led by the Divine Council, not only has spiritual authority but also significant political and economic power. The Church oversees the allocation of resources, distribution of wealth, and administration of justice in the country. The Church's influence extends to every aspect of life in Celestia, and its teachings shape the moral code and societal norms of the country.

Inquisitors and Enforcers: The Divine Council employs a group of elite enforcers known as the Inquisitors, who are charged with hunting down practitioners of arcane magic and maintaining the Council's authority. These highly trained and zealous individuals are feared and respected by the populace, as they wield immense power and authority to enforce the Council's laws. The Inquisitors are known for their relentless pursuit of heretics and their unwavering loyalty to the Divine Council. Inquisitors wear a White Lotus symbol to signify their “purity”

Underground Resistance: Despite the Divine Council's oppressive rule, there are those who oppose their authority and seek to challenge the ban on arcane magic. The underground resistance, known as the Order of the Silver Star, operates in secret, helping practitioners of arcane magic evade capture and plotting to overthrow the Divine Council. The Order of the Silver Star is made up of a diverse group of individuals, including former members of the Church, disillusioned citizens, and rogue practitioners of magic.
